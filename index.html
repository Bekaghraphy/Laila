🌸 What is Rhythmic Gymnastics?

💃

Sport + Art

Rhythmic gymnastics is a beautiful sport that mixes dance, ballet, acrobatics, and music together. Gymnasts move with elegance and power at the same time. Every routine tells a story through movement!

🎵

Music & Movement

Every routine is performed to music — sometimes classical, sometimes pop! The gymnast must match every movement perfectly to the rhythm. The music helps tell the story of the performance and express emotion.

🎪

Five Apparatus

Gymnasts use five special tools called "apparatus" — the ribbon, ball, hoop, clubs, and rope. Each one is different and needs its own special skills. Learning all five makes a gymnast truly amazing!

🏅

Olympic Sport

Rhythmic gymnastics has been part of the Olympic Games since 1984! Athletes from all over the world train for years to compete at the highest level. It is one of the most watched sports at the Olympics!

👯

Individual & Groups

You can compete alone — just you and your apparatus. Or you can be part of a group of 5 gymnasts who all move together in perfect harmony! Group routines are especially exciting and beautiful to watch.

⚡

Incredible Skills

Gymnasts need to be very flexible (like bending backwards to touch their head to their feet!), have perfect balance, fast reflexes to catch the apparatus, and the grace of a dancer. It takes years of practice!

📅 A Little History

1880s

The roots of rhythmic gymnastics began in Europe, mixing ideas from Swedish gymnastics, ballet movements, and the ideas of teachers like François Delsarte and Rudolf Bode about expressive movement.

1963

The International Gymnastics Federation (FIG) officially recognized rhythmic gymnastics as a sport! The first World Championship was held in Budapest, Hungary. The sport began to spread around the world.

1984

🏅 Big milestone! Rhythmic gymnastics made its debut at the Olympic Games in Los Angeles, USA. Canadian gymnast Lori Fung won the very first Olympic gold medal in the sport!

1996

Group gymnastics was added to the Olympics in Atlanta. Now teams of 5 gymnasts could compete together, making the sport even more exciting and spectacular to watch!

Today 🌟

Rhythmic gymnastics is now loved in over 100 countries! Stars like Sofia Raffaeli from Italy and Stiliana Nikolova from Bulgaria inspire millions of young girls around the world to start gymnastics.

📊 How Does Scoring Work?

🤸

Difficulty (D)

Judges look at how hard the moves are. Difficult jumps, balances, and rotations give higher scores. The harder the skill, the more points a gymnast earns!

🎨

Artistry (A)

How beautiful is the performance? Does the gymnast express emotion? Does the routine match the music? Artistry judges score the beauty and creativity of the whole performance.

⚙️

Execution (E)

This score is about perfection! If a gymnast drops the apparatus or makes a technical mistake, points are taken away. A clean routine with no mistakes scores highest!

🎯

Apparatus Handling

Judges watch closely how the gymnast uses the apparatus. They must throw it, catch it, and use it in creative ways. Dropping or losing control takes away points!

🎪 The 5 Apparatus

Every apparatus has its own personality, its own magic, and its own world of incredible moves. Learn everything about them!

```
🎀
Apparatus No.1

The Ribbon

The ribbon is perhaps the most beautiful and magical of all the apparatus! It is a long, shiny ribbon attached to a stick called a "wand." When a gymnast moves it through the air, it creates stunning spirals, snakes, and waves that look almost alive. The ribbon must NEVER stop moving or touch the floor during the routine.

The ribbon requires incredible coordination between the gymnast's body movements and her wrist movements. While her legs are doing difficult jumps and her body is bending, her wrist must keep the ribbon flowing in perfect shapes. It takes years of practice to master!

📏 Length: 6 meters ⚖️ Min. weight: 35g 🪡 Made of: Satin 🏆 Age: Used since 1963

✨ Famous Ribbon Moves

  • Snakes — the ribbon moves in an S-shape along the floor or in the air
  • Spirals — the ribbon makes tight coil shapes spinning in the air
  • Circles — the gymnast draws big and small circles with the ribbon
  • Figure-8s — the ribbon crosses itself in the shape of the number 8
  • Throws — the stick is thrown in the air while the ribbon flows freely
⭐ Difficulty to Master
⚾
Apparatus No.2

The Ball

The ball is the most graceful of all the apparatus! Unlike other sports where you grip a ball tightly, in rhythmic gymnastics you must never grip the ball — it should always rest gently on your hand, roll along your body, or float through the air. The relationship between gymnast and ball must look completely effortless and natural.

The ball can roll along the gymnast's arms, across her back, up her legs, and around her neck! It can be thrown high in the air while the gymnast performs a difficult jump or spin below. The secret is that the ball and the gymnast seem to belong together, moving as one.

📏 Diameter: 18–20 cm ⚖️ Weight: 400g min 🏀 Made of: Rubber 🎨 Color: Any color!

✨ Famous Ball Moves

  • Body rolls — the ball rolls smoothly from hand to hand across the body
  • Bounces — bouncing the ball in perfect rhythm with the music
  • High throws — launching the ball very high and catching gracefully
  • Balancing — balancing the ball on the back of the hand, head or neck
  • Floor rolls — rolling the ball across the floor and chasing it with the body
⭐ Difficulty to Master
🏸
Apparatus No.3

The Clubs

The clubs are the most exciting and dynamic apparatus! The gymnast uses TWO clubs at the same time — one in each hand. This makes it the most technically challenging apparatus because both hands must work independently while the body performs difficult gymnastics skills at the same time.

Clubs look a little like small bowling pins or bottles. They can be thrown in the air, spun, swapped from hand to hand, or juggled. When two clubs are thrown at the same time and the gymnast catches both — the crowd goes absolutely wild! The sound of the clubs spinning through the air is very satisfying.

📏 Length: 40–50 cm each ⚖️ Weight: 150g min each 🪵 Made of: Plastic/rubber ✌️ Quantity: 2 clubs used

✨ Famous Club Moves

  • Mills — both clubs spinning in small circles in front of the body
  • Asymmetric throws — throwing both clubs at different heights and angles
  • Juggling catches — one or both clubs thrown and caught behind the back
  • Body contact — rolling the clubs along the arms, legs and neck
  • Simultaneous throws — throwing both clubs up at the same time
⭐ Difficulty to Master
⭕
Apparatus No.4

The Hoop

The hoop is the most versatile apparatus — it can be used in SO many different ways! The gymnast can spin it like a wheel, jump through it like a portal, rotate it around her body, throw it in the air, or roll it across the floor. The hoop is also the largest apparatus, which makes moves look even more impressive and spectacular.

One of the most breathtaking moves is when the gymnast throws the hoop very high in the air, performs a full split jump while it's in the air, and catches it perfectly on the way down. The hoop adds a geometric element to the performance — circular shapes all around a human body in motion.

📏 Diameter: 80–90 cm ⚖️ Weight: 300g min 🔵 Made of: Plastic ⭕ Shape: Perfect circle

✨ Famous Hoop Moves

  • Spinning — rotating the hoop around the waist, neck, arm or leg
  • Jumping through — the gymnast jumps through the hoop in mid-air
  • Rolling — rolling the hoop along the floor and leaping over it
  • High throws — throwing the hoop way above the head and catching it
  • Pirouette catch — spinning in a pirouette and catching the falling hoop
⭐ Difficulty to Master
🪢
Apparatus No.5

The Rope

The rope might look simple — after all, everyone has jumped rope before! But in rhythmic gymnastics, the rope is used in ways that will completely amaze you. The gymnast can skip with it at incredible speed, swing it in giant circles, form shapes with it, or throw it in the air like a snake.

The rope has no handles — both ends are held directly. It must be proportional to the height of the gymnast. What makes the rope so special is the energy and joy it brings! Rope routines often feel the most alive and exciting because the gymnast seems to be flying and dancing all at once.

📏 Length: Proportional to height 🪢 Made of: Hemp or synthetic ✋ No handles! ⚡ Speed: Very fast movement

✨ Famous Rope Moves

  • Double-Dutch skipping — incredibly fast skipping with complex footwork
  • Big swings — swinging the rope in giant circles while doing leaps
  • Rope throws — throwing the entire rope in the air and catching it
  • Body wrapping — wrapping the rope around the body and legs
  • Folded rope jumps — folding the rope double and jumping at double speed
⭐ Difficulty to Master
