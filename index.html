What is Rhythmic Gymnastics?

💃

Sport + Art

Rhythmic gymnastics mixes dance, ballet, acrobatics, and music together. Every routine combines grace, control, flexibility, and storytelling.

🎵

Music & Movement

Every performance is built around music. The gymnast matches movement, rhythm, emotion, and expression in one flowing routine.

🎀

Five Apparatus

The sport uses ribbon, ball, hoop, clubs, and rope. Each apparatus needs its own style, control, and technical skill.

🏅

Olympic Sport

Rhythmic gymnastics has been part of the Olympic Games since 1984 and continues to inspire athletes all over the world.

👯

Individual & Groups

Gymnasts can compete individually or in groups, where perfect timing and team harmony create amazing performances.

⚡

Incredible Skills

It takes years of training to develop flexibility, balance, musicality, precision, and clean apparatus handling.

A Little History

1880s

The roots of rhythmic gymnastics started in Europe through expressive movement, ballet, and early gymnastics methods.

1963

The International Gymnastics Federation officially recognized rhythmic gymnastics and held the first World Championship.

1984

The sport made its Olympic debut in Los Angeles, becoming one of the most artistic events in the Games.

1996

Group rhythmic gymnastics joined the Olympics, adding spectacular team routines and synchronized movement.

Today

Rhythmic gymnastics is practiced in more than 100 countries and continues to grow through world championships, cups, and youth training.

The 5 Apparatus

Every apparatus has its own movement language, personality, and visual style.

🎀
Apparatus No. 1

Ribbon

The ribbon creates spirals, waves, circles, and flowing shapes. It must stay alive in the air and needs excellent wrist control and body coordination.

Length: 6 meters Weight: 35g min Material: Satin

Famous moves

  • Snakes
  • Spirals
  • Circles
  • Figure-8s
  • Throws and catches
Difficulty
🔵
Apparatus No. 2

Ball

The ball is all about softness, flow, rolling, and continuous connection with the gymnast’s body. It should never look forced.

Diameter: 18–20 cm Weight: 400g min Material: Rubber

Famous moves

  • Body rolls
  • Bounces
  • High throws
  • Balancing
  • Floor rolls
Difficulty
🥢
Apparatus No. 3

Clubs

Clubs are one of the most technical apparatus because both hands work separately while the gymnast performs jumps, balances, and turns.

Length: 40–50 cm Weight: 150g min each Quantity: 2 clubs

Famous moves

  • Mills
  • Asymmetric throws
  • Juggling catches
  • Body contact
  • Simultaneous throws
Difficulty
⭕
Apparatus No. 4

Hoop

The hoop adds strong circular geometry to routines and can be rolled, spun, jumped through, or thrown very high.

Diameter: 80–90 cm Weight: 300g min Material: Plastic

Famous moves

  • Spinning
  • Jumping through
  • Rolling
  • High throws
  • Pirouette catches
Difficulty
🪢
Apparatus No. 5

Rope

The rope gives routines speed, jump rhythm, and lively energy. It often creates some of the fastest-looking moments in rhythmic gymnastics.

Length: Proportional to gymnast No handles Fast movement

Famous moves

  • Skipping patterns
  • Big swings
  • Rope throws
  • Body wrapping
  • Folded rope jumps
Difficulty
